Sustainability in food encompasses various practices and principles aimed at ensuring the long-term viability of food production, distribution,and consumption while minimizing negative environmental, social, and economic impacts.

What is sustainable food?

  • Utilizing natural methods and avoiding synthetic pesticides and fertilizers to produce
    foodwhile minimizing harm to ecosystems.

  • Supporting local farmers and producers to reduce transportation emissions, promote
    regional food security, and strengthen local economies.

  • Choosing foods that are in season locally to reduce the energy required for storage,
    transportation, and refrigeration.

  • Minimizing food waste at all stages of the supply chain, from production and distribution to
    consumer consumption, through initiatives such as composting, recycling, and donation.

  • Ensuring humane treatment of animals raised for food production, including providing
    adequate living conditions, access to natural habitats, and freedom from unnecessary suffering.

  • Incorporating ecological principles into agricultural practices to enhance biodiversity,
    soil health, and ecosystem resilience while reducing the need for synthetic inputs.

  • Implementing efficient irrigation techniques, water recycling systems, and conservation
    practices to minimize water usage in food production.

  • Supporting fair labor practices and equitable wages for workers involved in food production,
    processing, and distribution, particularly in developing countries.

  • Promoting diets rich in plant-based foods such as fruits, vegetables, grains, legumes, and nuts,
    which typically have lower environmental footprints compared to animal-based foods.

  • Choosing seafood from responsibly managed fisheries or aquaculture operations
    that prioritize conservation of marine ecosystems and species diversity.
