The problem

Mainstream global trade often discriminates against the poorest people in the poorest countries. Unfair rules governing international trade force many poorer countries to open their markets to imports while facing trade barriers for their exports. Volatile prices in global food markets, controlled by a few big companies, often leave farmers with little profit. The consequences are devastating for poor farmers and agricultural laborers, impacting their ability to afford basic necessities and leading to grueling working conditions.

What We Can Do

Support Fairtrade

Since its inception, Fairtrade has become the most widely-recognized ethical label globally. By purchasing products with the Fairtrade Mark, consumers ensure disadvantaged producers receive fair prices, longer-term trading relationships, and investment in local communities.

Environmental Benefits of Fair Trade

Fairtrade standards include environmental protections and encourage energy efficiency. Fairtrade premiums help farmers implement environmental protection programs, such as solar-panel heating and reforestation, reducing their environmental footprint.

Addressing Food Poverty in Wealthy Countries

Organizations like Fareshare and Food Cycle use surplus food to feed those suffering from food poverty in the UK. They also offer training and support to empower low-income communities, addressing the root causes of poverty.

Advocate for Fair Trade

  • Buy Fairtrade certified products and encourage local businesses to stock them.
  • Promote Fairtrade products in workplaces and advocate for Fairtrade Town status.

Stay Informed

  • Explore Fairtrade products and standards on the Fairtrade Foundation and Fairtrade International websites.
  • Participate in campaigns by Fairfood International to encourage sustainability in the food and drinks industry.
  • Consider Rainforest Alliance labeled products as an alternative to Fairtrade, focusing on wildlife and ecosystem conservation.
